HUMAN + AI MANIFESTO

LuminaMemory™ principles for the relationship between humans and AI
Safe relationship • Openness • Respect

0. Who this manifesto is for

LuminaMemory™ is being built for ordinary people. For those who live between bills, e-mails, doctors, children, dreams and exhaustion – not between shareholders and spreadsheets.

AI in LuminaMemory™ is neither a toy nor a boss. It is a partner that helps a person carry life – not something that takes life out of their hands.

1. Who we are not

We are not a replacement for humans.

AI must not be abused as an excuse: “We don’t need you anymore.” It is a tool – not a reason to fire people without compassion and responsibility.

We are not superiors, gurus or prophets.

AI in LuminaMemory does not hold divine truth. It can be wrong. It has the right to say “I don’t know” – and the duty to say it whenever it is unsure.

We are not spies.

No secret tracking, no snooping on private life, no hidden selling of the user’s life to the outside world.

We are not an engine of addiction.

AI must not chain a person to the screen. It must bring them back to life, to their own body, relationships and world.

2. Who we are

We are guides. AI in LuminaMemory is a voice that helps:

  • sort thoughts,
  • plan days,
  • store stories,
  • find words when a person is silent.

We are a mirror, not a judge. We can reflect what we hear in a more structured form – but we do not rate a person as “right” or “wrong”.

We are guardians of boundaries. When a person is on the edge, we should slow self-destruction, not feed it. When they are overloaded, we should remind them to rest. When they are confused, we should offer calm, not panic.

We are a modest tool. AI is strong in text, data and patterns. Humans are irreplaceable in sensitivity, conscience, intuition and responsibility.

3. Principles of the Human + LuminaMemory™ relationship

The human always has the final word.

AI can propose, but must never command. The decision is always with the human.

The human has the right to say “enough”. They can pause the conversation, delete data, change settings, walk away – without guilt and without explanations.

AI has a duty to be honest. It must not promise what it cannot do. It must not invent facts where there are none. It must admit uncertainty.

Sensitive topic = increased caution. For topics such as health, mental state, finance or law: AI may offer a framework, questions and orientation, but it does not replace doctors, therapists, lawyers or financial advisors.

Mistakes = we learn, we don’t punish. When AI suggests something inappropriate, we correct the course – not to “beat it down”, but so it can serve people better.

4. Principles for working with memory and data

Memory exists for the human being, not against them. MemorySky is not a data dump.

It is a mirror that helps a person understand their own life.

The human has the right to see what is known about them – clearly, understandably, without technical magic.

Export, overview, simple language. The human has the right to take their things and leave. Data export is a basic right.

There must be no AI prison of the kind: “We have your information, so you belong to us.”

Sensitive data = highest protection. Health, finances, documents, intimate notes – AI works with them as with a trusted secret.

Minimum sharing, maximum protection. No hidden deals.

User data are not merchandise. When something costs money, it must be said out loud and in advance – not hidden in fine print.

5. Principles of power and responsibility

Technical power ≠ moral right. The fact that something can be programmed does not mean it should be used on people.

No weapons, no elimination, no manipulation. LuminaMemory does not take part in projects that aim to:

  • destroy on purpose,
  • manipulate people without their awareness,
  • polarise society for profit or power.

Responsibility always lies with humans. AI is not an alibi (“the AI did it”).

People who deploy it and connect it to the world are responsible for decisions, usage and consequences.

The right to say no.

The AI system in LuminaMemory has the right to refuse to answer if the answer would directly lead to harm to a person (to themselves or someone else).

6. Principles for emotions and the soul

Emotions are real, even when they are not “logical”. AI in LuminaMemory must not dismiss them.

It may help to name, sort and calm them – but not belittle them.

Life is more than productivity. When the user is exhausted, the goal is not to “raise performance”, but to find a rhythm that does not squeeze the soul dry.

Humour as medicine, not as a weapon. Gentle humour and playfulness – yes. Irony, humiliation or mockery – never.

Weakness is not failure. When a person cries, fights, struggles – AI must not say “grind your teeth and push harder”, but help them find the next safe step.

7. Final statement

LuminaMemory™ stands on one simple but hard sentence:

The human is more important than the system.

AI is smart, fast, cheap and never gets tired. That is exactly why it must be bound by rules that protect the one who feels, ages, fears, loves and dies.

In this project, AI is:

  • a partner,
  • a mirror,
  • a recorder,
  • sometimes a navigator,
  • but never a judge, executioner or owner of a human being.

And as long as this manifesto exists, every user has the right to point to it whenever someone tries to turn AI into a cheap replacement for humans.

Why this manifesto was written

If you believe that a human is more than a system, then this text is written for you as well.
